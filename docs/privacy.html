Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-05-28 · Last updated: 2026-05-28

This Privacy Policy describes how the GRECHKA application (the "App") collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use it. By installing or using the App, you confirm that you have read and accepted this Policy.

1. Who we are

The App is operated by the GRECHKA team (the "we", "us", "our"). The App is currently in prototype distribution and not yet published in any public store. For any privacy-related question or request you can reach us at platonipatov2@gmail.com.

2. Information we collect

Account information

When you sign in using a Google account, we receive your email address, public display name, and profile picture URL from Google's OAuth response. We do not receive your Google password or access any other Google data.

If you sign in using a built-in test account (the seeded roster shipped with prototype builds), we store only the email address and the display name you chose during sign-in.

Content you provide

The App stores the content you actively create or log:

  • chat messages you send to the in-app assistant;
  • logged meals (item name, weight, calories, macros, timestamp, meal slot);
  • recipes you create, including their titles, ingredients, steps, and any photo you attach;
  • your profile (name, age, weight, height, target weight, activity level, dietary goal, daily kcal and macro targets, water target);
  • water-intake entries and other manual logs you submit;
  • scanned barcode values and photos you attach to chat messages.

Device-side technical data

The App stores some technical state locally on your device for the App to function: a per-device anonymous identifier, the selected language and units, your subscription tier setting, and cached attachments. This data does not leave the device unless explicitly listed elsewhere in this Policy.

What we do not collect

We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. We do not request location, contacts, calendar, or microphone access without you explicitly initiating a feature that requires it (e.g. starting voice dictation). We do not place advertising trackers.

3. How we use your information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Providing the service. Calculating your daily totals, rendering your diary, suggesting recipes, walking you through cooking, and otherwise running the features you interact with.
  • Improving the product. Aggregated and de-identified usage data helps us understand which flows work and which need fixing.
  • Training and evaluating models. Your chat messages, logged meals, scanned products, and recipes may be used to train, evaluate, and fine-tune the AI models that power the assistant, and to develop new features. Whenever possible we de-identify content before doing so.
  • Building the recipe catalogue. Recipes you create may be incorporated, in whole or in part, into a shared recipe database used to recommend dishes to other users and to analyse food trends. Personally identifying details are removed before any such use.
  • Trend and analytics work. Aggregated nutrition and behavioural patterns (e.g. average daily kcal, popular ingredients) may be analysed to identify trends and to improve nutritional recommendations across the user base.
  • Authentication and security. Detecting abuse, preventing unauthorised access, and enforcing this Policy and our Terms.
By using the App, you grant us a worldwide, royalty-free, sublicensable, non-exclusive licence to host, store, reproduce, modify, create derivative works of, analyse, and use the content you submit through the App (including chat messages and recipes) for the purposes described above — including model training, product development, analytics, and the shared recipe catalogue. You retain ownership of your content.

4. Third-party services we share data with

The App relies on a small number of external services to function. Each is invoked only when the corresponding feature is used.

  • Google — for OAuth 2.0 sign-in. We receive your email, name, and avatar URL when you choose "Continue with Google". See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • OpenRouter — when you chat with the in-app assistant, the contents of your messages and the relevant diary context are sent to OpenRouter, which forwards them to the selected language model. See OpenRouter's Privacy Policy.
  • FatSecret — when you scan a barcode, the barcode digits are sent to FatSecret to look up the corresponding product. See FatSecret's terms.
  • Open Food Facts — barcode lookups are also queried against Open Food Facts and its sister databases (Pet Food, Products). See Open Food Facts Privacy Policy.
  • USDA FoodData Central — used as a fallback for barcode lookups. See USDA Privacy Policy.
  • Apple — push notifications, code signing, App Store distribution, and (optionally) HealthKit data on your device. See Apple's Privacy Policy.

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not share it with advertisers.

5. Where data is stored

Most of your data — your profile, diary, chat history, recipes, water entries, and cached attachments — is stored locally on your device. When the App connects to its own backend, your account identifier and the meals you log may be transmitted to the backend and stored there for synchronisation. Third-party services we contact store their own copies of the data we send them, governed by their respective policies.

6. Data retention

We keep your account information and the content you submit for as long as you maintain an account, and for a reasonable time afterwards as required to provide the service, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, or improve the App. You can request deletion of your account and associated content by writing to platonipatov2@gmail.com; we will respond within thirty (30) days.
